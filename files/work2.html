1 - Bellabeat Case Study

Bellabeat is a fictional manufacturer of high-tech wellness products for women- including a wellness app, 2 forms of digital activity tracking devices, a water bottle that tracks daily water intake, and a personalized wellness membership program.

In this case study, I analyzed general smart device usage trends and used these to inform Bellabeat marketing strategy.

Tools Used: Excel, R Studio, Tableau

Highlights: Cleaned and analyzed data with R; Encorporated additional research to ensure accurate analyses

View the full report here

Insights

Key Takeaways

Focus Marketing Content on Improvements in Physical Activity
Overall, there was approximately a 1,440 step increase in the median number of steps taken during the first 23 days of tracking. There was also an increase of 262 calories in the median number of calories burned in the first 23 days of tracking.

There was a positive correlation between number of steps taken and calories burned.
Previous studies have shown that using a smart device for physical activity tracking can lead to a short-term increase in physical activity.

Marketing content for Bellabeat’s tracking devices should thus focus on the potential physical improvements users could see when starting to track their activity and how this would benefit their health.

Target Marketing to Ages 36-55
The study “Determinants for Sustained Use of an Activity Tracker: Observational Study” found that one of the greatest indicators of continued physical activity tracker usage was age.
Figure 5 - Hermsen S, Moons J, Kerkhof P, Wiekens C and De Groot M., 2017

Participants who were aged 36-55 showed the longest usage of their activity trackers, even when accounting for gender. Thus, this subsection of the population should be targeted for marketing because the longer Bellabeat can keep users engaged, the more profit they can make from each user.

Tarket Marketing to “Contemplation” and “Preparation” Stages Using Keywords
The study “How Do We Engage With Activity Trackers? A Longitudinal Study of Habito” found that those who were in the “Contemplation” and “Preparation” stages of readiness had much higher adoption rates of the physical activity tracking application being looked at.

Table 1 - Gouveia, Rúben & Karapanos and Evangelos & Hassenzahl, Marc, 2015

Marketing should thus be targeted towards the kinds of potential users who may be in the “Contemplation” or “Preparation” stages of readiness for tracking in order to see long-term usage. There are likely certain keywords these potential users are searching online as they are thinking about taking the step towards becoming physically active - testing and capitalizing on these keywords will lead to long-term success.
